excerpt: 'Event cameras are neuromorphic sensors that asynchronously capture intensity changes, making them highly effective for fast motion and challenging lighting conditions. While Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) have revolutionized 3D scene representation, they remain limited when operating on blurred, low dynamic range (LDR) inputs. In this work, we present a novel framework that fuses blurred RGB frames with an accompanying event stream to reconstruct sharp, high-dynamic range (HDR) novel views. Specifically, we exploit the high temporal resolution of events to guide luma sharpening and dynamic range expansion, generating intermediate HDR image sequences. We generate a stack of sharp LDR images at different exposures through tone mapping, which are then integrated into an enhanced NeRF pipeline. This enables our model to learn richer radiance fields that recover fine details and handle extreme lighting variations more effectively than existing methods. Extensive experiments on real and simulated benchmarks show that our approach is competitive with state-of-the-art HDR and LDR NeRF methods and often outperforms them. This demonstrates its potential as a robust solution for generating high-quality novel views in fast-motion scenarios and challenging lighting conditions.'
